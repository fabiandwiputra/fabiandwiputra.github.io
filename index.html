On the Job Training Experience Report

During my On the Job Training (OJT) as a student of Light Vehicle Engineering, I had the opportunity to apply the knowledge and skills I learned at school in a real workshop environment. My training focused on several key areas: periodic maintenance, engine and clutch servicing, and air conditioning (AC) system service, including damage analysis.

  • Periodic Maintenance and Engine Service

    • I was involved in various periodic maintenance tasks such as changing engine oil, checking and replacing air and oil filters, inspecting brake systems, and rotating tires. One of the most valuable experiences was participating in an engine overhaul process. I helped in dismantling the engine block, cleaning engine components, inspecting cylinder walls and pistons, and reassembling the engine with new parts as needed. This activity improved my understanding of engine components and how to detect wear and damage. I also learned how to analyze engine problems such as unusual noises, oil leaks, loss of power, and overheating. These analyses were done using both manual inspections and diagnostic tools.

  • Clutch System Maintenance

    • In the clutch system section, I assisted in removing the transmission to access the clutch assembly. I helped replace worn-out clutch plates, pressure plates, and release bearings. I also learned how to inspect the flywheel surface for damage and measure clutch free-play and pedal height. Understanding the symptoms of clutch failure, such as slipping or difficulty shifting gears, became part of my daily observations.

  • Air Conditioning (AC) Service

    • I gained hands-on experience in servicing car AC systems. I learned how to replace the evaporator, clean the cooling system, and add refrigerant (freon) using proper tools. We also diagnosed AC problems such as weak airflow, unpleasant odors, and refrigerant leaks. In several cases, we used leak detectors and pressure gauges to pinpoint the exact issues. I also assisted in vacuuming the AC system before recharging it, which is an important step in maintaining system efficiency.
